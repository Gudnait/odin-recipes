Fake Rabbit Recipe

Description

The "fake rabbit," is a beloved Bolivian dish that belies its name. This hearty meal features thin, breaded beef cutlets, often seasoned with aji amarillo, a bright yellow chili pepper. The cutlets are pan-fried to a golden brown and then simmered in a rich, flavorful sauce. The name "fake rabbit" likely stems from the cutlets' resemblance to rabbit meat, but the taste is entirely its own, offering a satisfying blend of savory and slightly spicy flavors.

Ingredients

  • Thin beef cutlets
  • Aji amarillo (yellow chili pepper)
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Eggs
  • Flour
  • Oil for frying
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Vegetable broth
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Pound the beef cutlets to make them thinner.
  2. Season the cutlets with salt, pepper, and aji amarillo.
  3. Dredge the cutlets in flour, then egg, and finally breadcrumbs.
  4. Fry the breaded cutlets in hot oil until golden brown.
  5. Saut√© onions, and tomatoes in a pot.
  6. Add the fried cutlets to the pot along with vegetable broth and bay leaves.
  7. Simmer the mixture until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld.
  8. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve hot with rice or potatoes.
