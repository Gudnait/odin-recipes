Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with rich meat sauce, béchamel sauce, and cheese. The meat sauce is typically a hearty blend of ground beef or sausage, tomatoes, onions, and spices. Béchamel sauce, a creamy white sauce made from milk, butter, and flour, adds richness and smoothness to the dish. The lasagna is then baked in the oven until bubbly and golden brown, creating a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or sausage
  • Tomato sauce
  • Onion
  • Milk
  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Nutmeg
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  2. Brown the ground beef or sausage in a large skillet, then drain off excess grease.
  3. Add onion to the skillet and cook until softened.
  4. Stir in tomato sauce and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Make the béchamel sauce by melting butter in a saucepan, whisking in flour, and gradually adding milk, whisking constantly until smooth and thickened.
  6. Season the béchamel sauce with nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  7. Assemble the lasagna in a baking dish, layering noodles, meat sauce, béchamel sauce, ricotta cheese, and mozzarella cheese.
  8. Top with a final layer of mozzarella cheese and Parmesan cheese.
  9. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 30-40 minutes, or until bubbly and golden brown.
  10. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.
